philosophy

I think most people are familiar with works of Bernard Shaw, to quote a maxim from "Man and Superman": He who can, does. He who can not, teaches. Surely you have heard it in some form or context.

I feel it is important to toil in the mud, to fail in one's attempts and to make mistakes, because these experiences ensure that we are moving forward. Success is not only measured in how many times you meet your goals, but also by how many times you helped someone else reach their's.

I feel we have two main obligations in life, one of continuous learning and adapting with the changing times and the other of passing on our knowledge and wisdom to our successors.
